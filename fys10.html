ISO 22000


ISO 22000 is a Food Safety Management System that can be applied to any organization in the food chain, “farm to fork”.


A food safety management system greatly enhances customer satisfaction and confidence, given the increasing emphasis on quality and safety of food, food processing and ingredients sourcing.


This Standard applies to Food Safety, incorporating the widely used and proven Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Plan* (HACCP) principles and Good Manufacturing Principles addressed by The Prerequisite Programs in ISO 22000.


ISO 22000 does not have specific requirements for prerequisite programs (PRPs), but requires the organization to identify and apply relevant programs making it a flexible certification.


Many of the food processors manufacturers and suppliers use the ISO Technical specification ISO/TS 22002-1 to develop their PRP programs.


ISO 22000 is not a Global Food Safety Initiative (GFSI) benchmarked standard. This means that if your customer base or market is looking for a GFSI Recognized standard you should look at FSSC 22000, which is the most similar to ISO 22000 or one of the other GFSI recognized certification schemes.


Requirements


>> Effective Pre-requisite Programs in place to ensure a clean sanitary environment.

>> A Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Plan developed to identify, prevent and eliminate food safety hazards.

>> Traceability system to ensure product identification.

>> Documented procedures.

>> A well maintained internal audit program.

>> Established documented food safety management system processes to manage food safety throughout your organization - from management and business planning aspects to day to day communication and operations.


* HACCP-Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Point


This is an internationally recognized system for reducing the risk of safety hazards in food. A HACCP System requires that potential hazards are identified and controlled at specific points in the process. This includes biological, chemical or physical hazards.


Any company involved in the manufacturing, processing or handling of food products can use HACCP to minimize or eliminate food safety hazards in their product.


Advantage ISO 22000


>> Assures quality of food.

>> HACCP certification ensures greater productivity and minimal wastage.

>> Enhances brand image.

>> Improves internal processes needed to provide consistently safe food.

>> Ensures continual improvement of food safety management system.

>> Facilitates adequate control at all stages of the food supply chain and prevents food safety hazards.

>> Provides clear understanding of responsibility and accountability at all levels.
